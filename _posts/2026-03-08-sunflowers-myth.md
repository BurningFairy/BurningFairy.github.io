A myth from Greek mythology was once used to explain why sunflowers appear to follow the sun. The story tells of Clytie, a water nymph who fell deeply in love with Helios, the sun god. However, Helios did not return her affection and instead loved another woman. Heartbroken, Clytie spent her days watching Helios drive his chariot across the sky. She sat and gazed at the sun continuously, refusing to eat or drink. After nine days of watching the sun move across the sky, she became rooted to the ground and transformed into a sunflower. From then on, the flower was said to keep turning toward the sun as a reflection of her constant devotion to Helios. This myth was historically used to explain why sunflowers appear to track the sun’s movement across the sky.
