Sunflowers (specifically Helianthus annuus) were deeply significant
to the Aztecs for over 4,000 years, serving as sacred symbols of
their sun god and warrior culture, frequently appearing in ritual,
art, and daily life. Known as chimalxochitl (shield flower) in Nahuatl,
they were used for food, medicine, and to adorn temples.
